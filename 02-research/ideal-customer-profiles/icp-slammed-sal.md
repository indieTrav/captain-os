## The Person

**Name / Archetype:** Slammed Sal — the hands-on owner-operator who runs the floor *and* the books and has no time left over for "tech stuff."

**Age range:** 38–58

**Role / Title:** Owner / Owner-Operator / "the guy whose name is on the door." Often also the head chef, the closer, the bookkeeper, and the hiring manager.

**Industry:** Independent single-location restaurants — neighborhood Italian, family taqueria, diner, pizzeria, BBQ joint, café, ethnic family-run spot. 1–2 locations max. Not chains, not franchises, not "concept" groups with a marketing budget.

**Location:** Suburban and small-city US Main Streets and strip malls. Lives within 15 minutes of the restaurant. In-person, on-site, six days a week.

---

## Their Situation

**What do they do day-to-day?**
Sal is at the restaurant by 9am for deliveries, preps with the kitchen, works the lunch rush, does a cash drop, handles a no-show line cook, takes a call from a customer asking "are you open on Memorial Day?", and is still there at close counting the till. His "website," if it exists, is a half-finished Wix page from 2019, a Facebook page his nephew set up, or just a Google listing with the wrong hours. People call to ask the menu because they can't find it online. He knows he's losing the under-35 crowd who won't call — they just check Google, see no menu, and pick the place next door.

**Current income / revenue:**
$400k–$1.2M/year in food sales, but razor-thin margins — 3–8% net in a good year. Takes home $50k–$95k personally. A new website is a $30–$300/mo decision he weighs against a new fryer, a payroll bump, or his own paycheck. Price sensitivity is real, but so is the fear of looking unprofessional.

**Stage of their journey:**
Open 4–20 years. Survived COVID by white-knuckling takeout and a third-party delivery app that ate 30% of every order. Past the "will we make it" panic, now stuck in "why am I working 70 hours for this?" He's not trying to build an empire — he wants the place full, the regulars happy, and to stop bleeding money to apps and middlemen.

## Psychology

**Deepest desire:**
To be the spot the neighborhood is proud of — full tables, a line out the door on Friday, regulars who bring their kids. The website isn't about "digital transformation"; it's about not looking second-rate next to the new place down the street, and about owning his own customers instead of renting them from an app.

**Biggest fear:**
That he's slowly becoming invisible to anyone under 40 — that the people who'd love his food never even find him because they live on their phones and his place doesn't show up the way the competitors do. Underneath that: the fear of getting ripped off again by another "tech guy" who takes his money and leaves him with something broken he can't fix.

**What they believe about themselves:**
"I'm a food guy, not a computer guy." He's proud, competent, and skeptical — he's been burned by vendors before and assumes anyone selling him tech is overcharging him for something simple. He'll trust a person before a platform.

**Objections they'll raise:**
1. "I already have a Facebook page — why do I need a website?"
2. "The last guy I paid disappeared on me. How do I know you won't?"
3. "I don't have time to sit and learn another system."
4. "How much a month? For what? I can't justify that when I just need a new walk-in."
5. "Can I update the menu myself, or do I have to call you every time I change a price?"

## What to validate next

1. **The #1 pain.** Is it really delivery-app commissions, or is it the time/overwhelm of doing the site at all? Interview 5 owners and rank what they say first, unprompted.
2. **Willingness to pay.** Is this a one-time build ($X), a monthly DIY-builder fee ($30–80/mo), or a done-for-you retainer? Find the price that stops feeling like "another fryer decision."
3. **DIY vs. done-for-you.** Does Sal want to update the menu himself, or does he want to *never touch it*? This changes the whole product.
4. **Trust trigger.** What makes a skeptical, previously-burned owner say yes? Local referral? Money-back guarantee? Month-to-month, no contract?
5. **Where they actually search.** Confirm the real Google search phrases and which communities/podcasts they genuinely use before spending on content or ads.
