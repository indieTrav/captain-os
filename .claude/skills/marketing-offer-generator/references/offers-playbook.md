# Offers Playbook

A great offer is the lifeblood of a business. Master it and you can charge a premium, attract motivated buyers, and get paid in proportion to value created. Weak offers grind founders down with low-quality customers, thin margins, and price wars. The skill is making the value-to-price gap so wide that prospects feel stupid saying no.

Sourced from *$100M Offers* (Alex Hormozi).

## Core Principles

- **The offer is where business begins.** No offer → no business. Bad offer → negative profit. Decent → stagnation. Grand Slam Offer → freedom.
- **Starving Crowd > Offer Strength > Persuasion Skills.** Pick the market first.
- **Charge a premium.** Cutting price destroys perceived value, client investment, client results, your delivery margin, and customer quality. No strategic benefit to being second-cheapest.
- **Goal: most money, not most customers.** Build offers for egregious returns, not popularity.
- **Widen the value-to-price gap by raising value, not cutting price.**
- **Sell the destination, not the plane flight.** The prospect wants what the product gets them.
- **Commit to the niche.** Under $10M/year revenue, niching down makes more money. "Riches in the niches" is a pricing strategy.
- **Demand must exceed supply, always.** Satisfy demand fully and desire dies. Engineer permanent scarcity.
- **Fear of loss > desire for gain.** Build offers, scarcity, and urgency around what they lose, not just gain.

## The Value Equation

```
            Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value  =  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                   Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice
```

Raise the numerator or shrink the denominator:

- **Dream Outcome (↑)** — what they actually want. Biggest dream outcomes tap status gains in the eyes of others.
- **Perceived Likelihood (↑)** — certainty. Proof, social proof, guarantees, demonstrations. People pay for certainty.
- **Time Delay (↓)** — speed to first result. Fast beats free. Bake in immediate wins.
- **Effort & Sacrifice (↓)** — what they must do, learn, give up. The bottom of the equation is where the best companies compete — anyone can promise the top.

Reduce time delay and effort toward zero → value approaches infinite.

## Four Market Filters

Pick a market scoring well on all four:

1. **Massive Pain** — desperate need, not just want. "The pain is the pitch."
2. **Purchasing Power** — they have money.
3. **Easy to Target** — they cluster (associations, lists, subreddits, channels, conferences).
4. **Growing** — markets that grow are tailwinds; declining ones fight every effort.

Three perennial markets: **Health, Wealth, Relationships.**

## The Grand Slam Offer Build (5 steps)

Produces an offer that can't be price-compared:

1. **Identify Dream Outcome.** Specific, tangible, bigger than the membership ("Lose 20lbs in 6 weeks" > "join my bootcamp").
2. **List Problems.** Exhaustive: every obstacle, limiting belief, and "next problem" before *and after* using the product.
3. **List Solutions.** Reframe each problem as a solution you provide.
4. **Create Delivery Vehicles.** Brainstorm every possible way to deliver each solution (group call, 1:1, async video, software, checklist, in-person). Diverge wide.
5. **Trim & Stack.** Keep only the highest-value / lowest-cost-to-deliver items. Bundle into a stack where each item has its own perceived value.

Final shape: differentiated promise + value stack + premium price + unbeatable guarantee + a money model that funds new-customer acquisition.

## M-A-G-I-C Offer Naming

Use 3–5 of these. Shorter and punchier wins.

- **M — Magnet.** Reason why. *Free, 88% Off, Grand Opening, Back-to-School, Anniversary, New Management.*
- **A — Avatar.** Who it's for. *Brooklyn Busy Executives, Lakeway Moms, Salon Owners, Retired Athletes.* Hyper-local where possible.
- **G — Goal.** The outcome. *Pain Free, Celebrity Smile, 1st Place, Never Out of Breath, $10k, First Client.*
- **I — Interval.** Timeframe. *4 Hour, 21 Day, 6 Week, 90 Day.* Skip if making a quantified income/weight claim (ad platforms flag).
- **C — Container.** The wrapper. *Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Intensive, Masterclass, Sprint, Accelerator, Reset, Liftoff, Comeback, Cheatcode.*

Rhyme or alliterate when natural; never force. Each bonus gets its own M-A-G-I-C-style name.

## Four Guarantee Types

Broadest-appeal → highest-stakes:

1. **Unconditional** — "Try it. Not satisfied? Full refund, no questions." Strongest for conversion; expect more refund requests. Best for lower-ticket B2C.
2. **Conditional** — Refund only if buyer did X, Y, Z (the things tied to success). Better: "We pay you $X if it doesn't work." Use for higher-ticket and B2B.
3. **Anti-Guarantee** — "All sales final." Needs a credible reason-why (often a damaging admission about exclusivity / once-seen-cannot-be-unseen). Works for exclusive / high-reveal-value offers.
4. **Implied (Performance / Revshare)** — "If you don't win, I don't get paid." Needs tracking and trust. Best alignment; weeds out low-performers.

Stack guarantees freely (unconditional satisfaction + conditional outcome-based).

### Specific Guarantee Patterns

- **Outsized Refund** — "Double your money back if X doesn't happen."
- **Service Guarantee** — "We keep working for free until X." Cap with conditions or duration.
- **Modified Service Guarantee** — "If X isn't achieved, another Y-period free." Bounds downside.
- **Credit-Based** — Refund as credit toward any other service.
- **Personal Service** — 1-on-1 until X result. Conditional on them doing the work.
- **Hotel + Airfare Perks** — For workshops/events: reimburse ancillary costs if no value. Feels huge, costs little.
- **Wage-Payment** — Pay their hourly rate if call wasn't useful. Reads bold; almost no one claims.
- **Release of Service** — Void the commitment. Costs little if you don't enforce contracts.
- **Delayed Second Payment** — Don't bill second installment until they hit first outcome.
- **First Outcome / Ancillary Cost** — "We pay your ad spend until your first sale."

Tips: name the guarantee cool ("Club a Baby Seal Guarantee" out-converts "30-Day Satisfaction"). Match the prospect's psychological investment with better-than-money-back terms.

## Three Scarcity Caps

Limit supply structurally, not as tactic:

1. **Total Business Cap** — "We only ever serve X clients. Period."
2. **Growth Rate Cap** — "Only X new clients per week." Creates ongoing queue.
3. **Cohort Cap** — "100 clients per cohort, 4 cohorts per year. Doors open then close."

Once-a-month is the sweet-spot cadence for scarcity events.

## Four Urgency Plays

Scarcity = quantity. Urgency = time.

1. **Rolling Cohorts** — fixed-date enrollment closes; next is months out.
2. **Rolling Seasonal Urgency** — real countdown timers, dated promotions ("Spring Promo ends Friday"). Must be real or credibility dies.
3. **Pricing / Bonus-Based Urgency** — "Get in today, lock in [discount / bonus / payment plan]." Lets year-round businesses drive action.
4. **Exploding Opportunity** — the arbitrage itself decays (early-mover advantage, market correction, platform saturation). Each day of delay = real cost.

## Bonus Stack (11-step presentation)

1. Always offer bonuses (the trimmed stack from Grand Slam build).
2. Give each a benefit-loaded name.
3. Tell them: how it relates to their issue, what it is, how you discovered/created it, how it specifically improves their life (faster, easier, less effort — the value equation).
4. Provide proof: stat, past client, or personal experience.
5. Paint a vivid mental image of them using it.
6. Ascribe a price tag to each bonus; justify it.
7. Tools and checklists beat additional trainings (lower effort, same value).
8. Each bonus addresses a specific concern or obstacle.
9. Each bonus solves the *next* problem they'll hit, before they hit it.
10. Combined bonus value should eclipse the core offer.
11. Stack scarcity or urgency onto specific bonuses to amplify.

Plus:
- **Add bonuses instead of discounting** to close. Discounts train negotiability.
- **Use other businesses' products as bonuses.** Adjacent (non-competing) businesses give free service for exposure.
- **Stacked bonuses outperform the same offer presented as one thing.**
- **Maintain a bonus vault.** Every workshop, webinar, event, interview is a future bonus asset.

## Pricing Tactics

- **Charge as high as you can say without cracking a smile.** (Dan Kennedy). Most founders leave 2–5x on the table.
- **Anchor far above market, then justify with value.** A price that screams "different category" forces the prospect to ask *what's different*.
- **Never raise prices silently.** Announce — fence-sitters convert at the threat of paying more later.
- **Don't pre-price.** Build value first, *then* set the price.

## Value Creation Tactics

- **Frame benefits as third-party status gains.** "Your golf buddies' jaws drop when your ball flies 40 yards past theirs."
- **Engineer fast wins.** Get the prospect to a visible small result early.
- **Fast beats free.** Competing against free? Double down on speed.
- **Try psychological solutions before logical ones.** Logical ones have been tried (mirrors in elevators beat faster elevators).
- **Create flow, monetize flow, then add friction.** Generate demand first, refine the offer to be more profitable second.

## Scarcity Tactics

- **Sell out consistently** rather than over-order and miss. A miss kills scarcity strategy permanently.
- **Limit 1-on-1 access at the top tier.** Tiny number of high-priced direct-access slots.
- **"Once you leave, you can never return."** Makes existing customers think hard about churning.
- **The Chanel pattern.** 1–2 of each piece per store; every item is the last or second-to-last.

## Urgency Tactics

- **Engineer the last-4-hours bump.** 50–60% of campaign sales happen in the last 3% of the window.
- **For local/repeat businesses, vary the wrapper monthly.** Same core service, new dated promotion.
- **Late-stage cohort upsell.** Mid-cohort prospect: offer either expedited onboarding *or* sweetened deal (extended payment, more onboarding time) tied to next cohort.

## Naming & Offer Fatigue

When ads fatigue, change in this order (lightest → heaviest operationally):

1. Creative (images/video).
2. Body copy.
3. Headline (the offer's wrapper).
4. Duration ("6-Week" → "28-Day").
5. Enhancer (free / discount component).
6. Monetization structure (payment terms, price ladder).

Same core offer, different wrapper. Reach the bottom only after exhausting lighter changes.

## Decision Heuristics

- **Under $10M/year → niche down further.** Above that, broaden based on TAM.
- **Low-ticket B2C → unconditional guarantee.** High-ticket / B2B → conditional, specific guarantee.
- **Quantified income/weight claim + duration in name → ad platforms reject.** Drop one.
- **"Would you pay?" hypothetically → meaningless.** Only pre-orders, deposits, or payment validate.
- **Sell out before you stock out.** Always fewer slots than you can sell.
- **Once-a-month is the sweet spot for scarcity events.** More → cheapened; less → forgotten.
- **Competing against free → optimize for speed.**

## Common Mistakes

- **Copying competitors' pricing and going slightly below.** The competitors you're copying are broke.
- **Pricing for "market efficiency"** (just above cost). Building a job that pays barely above survival.
- **Discounting to close.** Trains negotiability; destroys delivery margin. Add bonuses instead.
- **Lazy naming.** Same offer with a half-assed name can 10x worse conversion than M-A-G-I-C name.
- **Trying to keep more customers when you should filter harder.** Cheap customers are most demanding, least satisfied.
- **Solving logically when psychological solutions exist.**
- **Refusing the sale over a single sticking point.** Concede on a non-core point and close.
- **Fake urgency / fake scarcity.** Credibility dies the moment prospect notices.
- **Niche-slapping yourself out** — picking a niche then bailing before figuring it out.

## Vocabulary

- **Grand Slam Offer** — Differentiated offer combining promise + stack + premium price + unbeatable guarantee + acquisition-funding money model.
- **Starving Crowd** — Market with massive urgent pain, purchasing power, easy targeting, growing trend.
- **Value Equation** — `(Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)`.
- **M-A-G-I-C** — Magnet · Avatar · Goal · Interval · Container.
- **Damaging Admission** — A vulnerability stated openly to justify a position ("all sales final because once you see this you can't un-see it"). Builds credibility.
- **Honest Scarcity** — Structurally enforced limits (capped slots, capped cohort).
- **Implied Guarantee** — Performance / revshare / profit-share: "If I don't perform, I don't get paid."
- **Hormozi's Law** — "The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make."
