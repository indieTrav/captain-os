# Offers Playbook

> A great offer is the lifeblood of a business. Master this and you can charge a premium, attract motivated buyers, sell against entrenched competitors, and get paid in proportion to the value you create — not the market average. Weak offers grind you down with low-quality customers, thin margins, and competitive pricing wars. The skill is making the value-to-price gap so wide that a prospect feels stupid saying no.

## Core Principles

- **The offer is where business begins.** No offer → no business. Bad offer → negative profit. Decent offer → stagnation. Grand Slam Offer → freedom. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Starving Crowd > Offer Strength > Persuasion Skills.** If the market is desperate enough, mediocre offers and persuasion still print money. If the market is dead, the best offer in the world won't save you. Pick the market first. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Charge a premium.** Cutting price destroys perceived value, client emotional investment, client results, your margin to deliver well, and the kind of customers you attract. There is no strategic benefit to being the second cheapest. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Goal: most money, not most customers.** Business is a value exchange for money. Pricing for market efficiency means pricing for survival. Build offers that produce egregious returns, not popularity. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Widen the value-to-price gap by raising value, not cutting price.** Lowering price is the lazy way to widen the gap and almost always the wrong move. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Sell the destination, not the plane flight.** Your prospect doesn't want your product — they want what your product gets them. Sell the dream outcome. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Commit to the niche.** Under $10M/year revenue, niching down makes you more money. Under 99.6% of businesses are here. "Riches are in the niches" is not a slogan; it's a pricing strategy. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Demand must exceed supply, always.** If you satisfy demand fully, desire dies. Engineer scarcity into the offer permanently — sell fewer units than you can. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Fear of loss is stronger than desire for gain.** Build offers, scarcity, and urgency around what they stand to _lose_, not just what they'll gain. — [[100M Offers]]

## Frameworks

### The Value Equation — [[100M Offers]]

```
            Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value  =  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
              Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice
```

To increase value, raise the numerator or shrink the denominator:

- **Dream Outcome** (↑) — what they actually want. The destination, not the vehicle. The biggest dream outcomes tap status gains in the eyes of others.
- **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** (↑) — certainty. Proof, social proof, guarantees, demonstrations. People pay for certainty.
- **Time Delay** (↓) — speed to first result. Fast beats free. Bake immediate wins into the offer.
- **Effort & Sacrifice** (↓) — what they have to do, learn, give up. The bottom of the equation is where the best companies compete, because anyone can promise the top.

If you reduce time delay and effort to zero, value approaches infinite.

### The Four Market Filters — [[100M Offers]]

Pick a market that scores well on all four:

1. **Massive Pain** — they don't just want, they desperately need. "The pain is the pitch."
2. **Purchasing Power** — they have money to spend.
3. **Easy to Target** — they cluster in identifiable places (associations, mailing lists, subreddits, channels, conferences). If they're needles in a haystack, you can't reach them.
4. **Growing** — markets that grow are tailwinds; declining markets are headwinds against every effort.

The three perennial markets that always exist: **Health, Wealth, Relationships**.

### The Grand Slam Offer Build — [[100M Offers]]

A five-step build that produces an offer that can't be price-compared to the competition:

1. **Identify Dream Outcome.** Make it specific, tangible, and bigger than the membership/service ("Lose 20lbs in 6 weeks" beats "join my bootcamp").
2. **List Problems.** Every obstacle, limiting belief, and "next problem" the prospect will hit before _and after_ using your product. Be exhaustive — each problem is an opportunity to add value.
3. **List Solutions.** Reframe each problem as a solution you provide.
4. **Create Delivery Vehicles.** For each solution, brainstorm every possible way you could deliver it (group call, 1:1, async video, software, checklist, in-person, etc.). Use divergent thinking — generate, then trim.
5. **Trim & Stack.** Keep only the highest value / lowest cost-to-deliver items. Bundle them into a stack where each item has its own perceived value.

The full offer name: a differentiated promise + value stack + premium price + unbeatable guarantee + a money model that funds new customer acquisition.

### M-A-G-I-C Offer Naming — [[100M Offers]]

Use 3–5 of these in any offer name. The shorter and punchier the better.

- **M — Magnet** (the "reason why" — _Free, 88% Off, Grand Opening, Back-to-School, Spring, Anniversary, New Management_)
- **A — Avatar** (who it's for — _Brooklyn Busy Executives, Lakeway Moms, Salon Owners, Retired Athletes_). When local, go hyper-local (a sub-market, not the city).
- **G — Goal** (the outcome — _Pain Free, Celebrity Smile, 1st Place, Never Out of Breath, $10k, First Client_)
- **I — Interval** (the timeframe — _4 Hour, 21 Day, 6 Week, 90 Day_). Skip if making a quantified income/weight claim — ad platforms will flag it.
- **C — Container** (the wrapper — _Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Intensive, Masterclass, Sprint, Accelerator, Reset, Liftoff, Comeback, Cheatcode_)

Rhyme or alliterate if natural; never force it. Each bonus inside the offer gets its own M-A-G-I-C-style name.

### The Four Guarantee Types — [[100M Offers]]

In rough order from broadest-appeal to highest-stakes:

1. **Unconditional** — "Try it. If you're not satisfied, get a full refund, no questions." Strongest for conversion; expect more refund requests. Better for lower-ticket B2C.
2. **Conditional** — Refund only if the prospect did X, Y, Z (the things tied to success). Better than money back: "We pay you $X if it doesn't work." Use for higher-ticket and B2B.
3. **Anti-Guarantee** — "All sales are final." Requires a credible "reason why" (often a damaging admission about exclusivity/once-seen-cannot-be-unseen). Works for very exclusive / high-value-once-revealed offers.
4. **Implied (Performance / Revshare)** — "If you don't win, I don't get paid." Requires tracking transparency and trust. Aligns incentives perfectly; weeds out low-performers.

You can **stack guarantees** (unconditional satisfaction + conditional outcome-based).

### The Three Scarcity Caps — [[100M Offers]]

Limit supply structurally, not as a tactic:

1. **Total Business Cap** — "We only ever serve X clients. Period." (e.g. "agency only services 25 customers total")
2. **Growth Rate Cap** — "We only accept X new clients per week." Creates a permanent ongoing queue.
3. **Cohort Cap** — "We take 100 clients per cohort, 4 cohorts per year. Doors open, then close."

Once-a-month is the sweet-spot cadence for scarcity events.

### The Four Urgency Plays — [[100M Offers]]

Scarcity = function of quantity. Urgency = function of time.

1. **Rolling Cohorts** — fixed-date enrollment closes; next one is months out.
2. **Rolling Seasonal Urgency** — real countdown timers and dated promotions ("Spring Promo ends Friday"). Must be real or you lose credibility.
3. **Pricing / Bonus-Based Urgency** — "Get in today and lock in [discount / extra bonus / payment plan]." Lets year-round businesses still drive action.
4. **Exploding Opportunity** — the arbitrage itself decays over time (early-mover advantage, market correction, platform saturation). Each day of delay = real cost.

## Tactics & Plays

### Pricing

- **Charge as high as you can say without cracking a smile.** (Dan Kennedy) Most founders are leaving 2-5x on the table. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Anchor far above market, then justify with value.** A price that screams "different category" forces the prospect to ask _what's different_, which opens the door to the value pitch. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Never raise prices silently.** Clean the pipeline with a price-change announcement — fence-sitters convert at the threat of paying more later. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Don't pre-price.** Build value first, _then_ set the price. The whole game is widening the gap. — [[100M Offers]]

### Market & Niche

- **Pain is the pitch.** Use the prospect's exact pain language in the copy. The point of good persuasion is for the prospect to _feel understood_. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Find pre-aggregated audiences.** Mailing lists, associations, social groups, channels they all watch. If they cluster, you can target them cheaply. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Sell into Health, Wealth, or Relationships.** These three markets never go away. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Commit, then iterate.** Once you pick the niche, don't bail when it gets hard. Quitting prematurely is the most common pre-mistake. — [[100M Offers]]

### Value Creation

- **Frame benefits as third-party status gains.** "Your golf buddies' jaws will drop when your ball flies 40 yards past theirs." — [[100M Offers]]
- **Engineer fast wins.** Get the prospect to a small visible result early so they know they're on the right path. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Fast beats free.** When competing against free, double down on speed. (Uber, FedEx, MVD-vs-DMV.) — [[100M Offers]]
- **Try psychological solutions before logical ones.** The logical solutions have already been tried. Add mirrors to elevators instead of making elevators faster. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Create flow, monetize flow, then add friction.** Generate demand first, then refine the offer to be more profitable. — [[100M Offers]]

### Scarcity

- **Sell out consistently rather than over-order and fail at scarcity.** A miss kills the strategy permanently. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Limit 1-on-1 access at the top tier.** "Extreme scarcity" — sell a tiny number of high-priced direct-access slots. The cream of the crop will take them. — [[100M Offers]]
- **"Once you leave, you can never return."** Makes existing customers think hard about churning. Works best with smaller groups. — [[100M Offers]]
- **The Chanel pattern.** Only 1–2 of each piece per store — every item is the last or second-to-last. Drives impulse-to-purchase conversion. — [[100M Offers]]

### Urgency

- **Engineer the last-4-hours bump.** 50–60% of campaign sales happen in the last 3% of the window. Tell prospects exactly when the door closes. — [[100M Offers]]
- **For local businesses, vary the wrapper monthly.** Same core service, new dated promotion — keeps creative fresh and urgency real. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Late-stage upsell during cohort breaks.** If a prospect arrives mid-cohort, offer them either an expedited onboarding _or_ a sweetened deal (extended payment plan, more time to onboard team) tied to the next cohort. — [[100M Offers]]

### Bonuses

The eleven-step bonus presentation — [[100M Offers]]:

1. Always offer bonuses (use the trimmed stack from the Grand Slam build).
2. Give each bonus a benefit-loaded name.
3. Tell them: how it relates to their issue, what it is, how you discovered/created it, and how it specifically improves their life (faster, easier, less effort — the value equation).
4. Provide proof: stat, past client, or personal experience.
5. Paint a vivid mental image of them already using it.
6. Always ascribe a price tag to each bonus, then justify it.
7. Tools and checklists beat additional trainings (lower effort, same value).
8. Each bonus addresses a specific concern or obstacle in the prospect's mind.
9. Each bonus solves the _next_ problem they'll hit, before they hit it.
10. The combined value of bonuses should eclipse the value of the core offer.
11. Stack scarcity or urgency onto specific bonuses to amplify.

Plus:

- **Add bonuses instead of discounting** to close. Discounting teaches prospects your prices are negotiable. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Use other businesses' products as bonuses.** Adjacent (non-competing) businesses will give you free service for exposure to your customers — free marketing for them, free bonus value for you. — [[100M Offers]]
- **A single offer broken into stacked bonuses outperforms the same offer presented as one thing.** — [[100M Offers]]
- **Maintain a bonus vault.** Every workshop, webinar, event, interview becomes a future bonus asset. — [[100M Offers]]

### Guarantees

Specific guarantee patterns — [[100M Offers]]:

- **Outsized Refund** — "Double your money back if X doesn't happen."
- **Service Guarantee** — "We keep working for free until X is achieved." Powerful, but cap with conditions or a duration.
- **Modified Service Guarantee** — "If X isn't achieved, we give you another Y-period of service free." Bounds your downside.
- **Credit-Based** — Refund as credit toward any other service. Maintains goodwill, retains revenue.
- **Personal Service** — 1-on-1 until X result. Conditional on them doing the work.
- **Hotel + Airfare Perks** — For workshops/events: reimburse ancillary costs if no value. Feels huge, costs little.
- **Wage-Payment** — Pay their hourly rate if a call wasn't useful. No one calculates and claims it; reads as bold.
- **Release of Service** — Void the commitment / contract. Costs little if you don't enforce contracts anyway.
- **Delayed Second Payment** — Don't bill the second payment until they hit a first outcome (first sale, first 5 lbs lost, website live).
- **First Outcome / Ancillary Cost** — "We pay your ad spend until you make your first sale."

Plus:

- **Name the guarantee something cool.** "Club a Baby Seal Guarantee" out-converts "30-Day Satisfaction Guarantee." — [[100M Offers]]
- **Match the prospect's psychological investment.** Better-than-money-back guarantees signal you've matched their risk. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Performance / revshare is the most aligned setup of all** — if collection and tracking can be solved. — [[100M Offers]]

### Naming & Offer Fatigue

When ads fatigue, change in this order (lightest → heaviest operationally) — [[100M Offers]]:

1. Creative (images/video in the ad)
2. Body copy
3. Headline (the offer's "wrapper")
4. Duration ("6-Week" → "28-Day")
5. Enhancer (free / discount component)
6. Monetization structure (payment terms, price ladder)

Don't change the entire business — same core offer, different wrapper. Reach the bottom of the list only after exhausting the lighter changes.

## Decision Heuristics

- **Under $10M/year → niche down further.** Above that, broadening may make sense based on total addressable market (TAM). — [[100M Offers]]
- **Low-ticket B2C → unconditional guarantee.** High-ticket / B2B → conditional, specific guarantee. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Quantifiable income/weight claim + duration in name → ad platforms will reject.** Drop the duration or drop the claim. — [[100M Offers]]
- **If a prospect asks "would you pay?" hypothetically → meaningless.** Only pre-orders, deposits, or payment are validation. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Sell out before you stock out.** Always have fewer slots than you think you can sell — protects future scarcity credibility. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Once-a-month is the sweet spot for scarcity events.** More often → cheapened; less often → forgotten. — [[100M Offers]]
- **If competing against a free alternative → optimize for speed.** — [[100M Offers]]

## Common Mistakes

- **Copying competitors' pricing and going slightly below.** The competitors you're copying are broke — why copy them? — [[100M Offers]]
- **Pricing for "market efficiency"** (just above cost). You're building a job that pays barely above survival. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Discounting to close.** Teaches prospects your prices are negotiable; destroys margin for fulfillment. Add bonuses instead. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Lazy naming.** Same offer with a half-assed name can 10x worse conversion than with a M-A-G-I-C name. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Trying to keep more customers when you should be filtering harder.** Cheap customers are the most demanding and the least satisfied. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Solving problems logically when psychological solutions exist.** The logical solutions have already been tried. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Refusing the sale over a single sticking point.** Concede on a non-core point and close — adding an "eating out guide" closed a sale that would otherwise have died. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Fake urgency / fake scarcity.** Loses credibility the moment a prospect notices. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Niche-slapping yourself out** — picking a niche, then bailing on it before figuring it out. — [[100M Offers]]

## Vocabulary

- **Grand Slam Offer** — An offer differentiated enough that it can't be price-compared, combining a compelling promise, value stack, premium price, unbeatable guarantee, and a money model that funds new customer acquisition. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Starving Crowd** — A market with massive urgent pain, purchasing power, easy targeting, and a growing trend. The single biggest leverage point. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Niche Slap** — Forcing yourself (or a coachee) to commit to a single narrow audience. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Value Equation** — `(Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)`. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Dream Outcome** — What the prospect actually wants. The destination, not the vehicle. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** — The prospect's belief that _they_ will achieve the result if they buy. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Total Addressable Market (TAM)** — The maximum revenue available if 100% of the market bought. Determines when to broaden vs. stay niched. — [[100M Offers]]
- **M-A-G-I-C Formula** — Magnet, Avatar, Goal, Interval, Container — the five components of a high-converting offer name. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Hormozi's Law** — "The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make." — [[100M Offers]]
- **Damaging Admission** — A vulnerability you openly state to justify a position (e.g. "all sales are final because once you see this you can't un-see it"). Builds credibility. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Honest Scarcity** — Real, structurally enforced limits (capped slots, capped cohort size). The most ethical form. — [[100M Offers]]
- **Implied Guarantee** — Performance, revshare, or profit-share — "If I don't perform, I don't get paid." — [[100M Offers]]
